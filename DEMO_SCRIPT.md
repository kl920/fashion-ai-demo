## Handling Questions

### Expected Questions & Answers

**Q: "Can't big brands just build this themselves?"**
> "They could, but they won't. Large brands focus on their core business, not R&D for internal tools. Mid-market brands, which are 80% of our target market, definitely can't. By the time larger players consider it, we'll have years of fashion-specific data and integrations they can't quickly replicate. Plus, our exit strategy includes acquisition by platforms like Shopify or Adobe."

**Q: "How accurate is the garment representation?"**
> "Current AI is excellent for overall style and presentation, reaching about 85-90% accuracy for simple garments. Complex details like intricate patterns are improving rapidly. For most use cases - e-commerce, social media, concept testing - current quality is more than sufficient. And it improves monthly as AI models advance."

**Q: "What about model consistency?"**
> "Great question. For now, each generation is unique. We're working on consistent model faces across generations - a feature that will significantly increase value. This is on our roadmap for Q3."

**Q: "Isn't this replacing human models?"**
> "We see it as complementary. Brands still use human models for hero shots and brand campaigns. This is for the hundreds of SKU images, seasonal variations, market testing, and regional personalization that weren't economically viable before. We're enabling imagery that wouldn't exist otherwise."

**Q: "What about copyright and AI ethics?"**
> "Excellent and important question. Generated images are owned by the user. We use licensed AI models. For commercial use, we advise clients on proper usage rights. As for ethics, we build tools - how they're used is up to users within legal bounds, similar to Photoshop or any creative tool."

**Q: "Why won't Adobe/Shopify just add this?"**
> "They will eventually - that's exactly our exit strategy. We're building the specialized fashion solution they'll want to acquire rather than build. Think of how Shopify acquired dozens of specific e-commerce tools. Our window is 2-3 years to become the category leader, then exit to a platform."

**Q: "How do you make money?"**
